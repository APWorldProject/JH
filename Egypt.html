About Egypt

The Tunisia plight was felt throughout the Arab World, especially in Egypt. Although the death of Mohammed Bouazizi represented a symbol for the entirety of the Arab Spring, another death proved to be an important symbolic catalyst for revolution in Egypt. A young Egyptian man, Khaled Said, was beaten to death by two police officers after being dragged out of a cybercafé in Alexandria. Images and videos of his death were quickly spread online and a movement quickly formed with the slogan, “We are all Khaled Said”. All Egyptians were able to resonate with Said. He had not been a political dissident nor an Islamic radical, but rather a young Egyptian “everyman” victimized by the police. He served as a symbol of all Egyptians being marginalized by people in authority and them not having a care in the world for their well being. Eventually President Hosni Mubarak lost power through a coup d'etat and Mohammed Morsi was elected in 2012.Egypt has the largest population of any Arab state for that reason the revolts and protests in the streets of Egypt were heard worldwide.
